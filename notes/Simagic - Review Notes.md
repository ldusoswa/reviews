## Official Marketing Claims & Specifications

"New base, New Standards. Evo is not just a slogan. An all-new generation of wheelbase."

- "Lighter weight, reduced inertia"
- "Sharper, smoother, and more precise force feedback"

#### Zero-Cogging Tech
- "Fine tuned to perfection"
- "Signature motor design offers highly consistent feedback"
- **Reviewer Note**: "Never really subscribed to much" - didn't feel cogging on older Alphas

#### Dynamic Force Domain Filter Algorithm (DFDF)
- "Optimized to accompany the new motor"
- "Giving extra FFB fidelity, further improving handling"

#### 21-Bit Encoder
- "Lower inertia, better modularity"
- "Precision unlike any base we've produced before"
- Higher fidelity and road feel than ever
- **Reviewer Note**: "If anyone can tell the difference between 21 bit and 23 bit I'd be very very surprised"

#### Open Ecosystem
- "Further improved compatibility for third-party devices"
- **Reviewer Note**: Loves this approach, compares favorably to Ascher

#### Dual-Mode Connectivity
- "Reliable and versatile"
- "Built to withstand hours upon hours of hard racing"

#### Magnetic Socket Design
- "A Connection That clicks"
- "Maximising contact security"

#### Patent Slip Ring Design
- "Built-in wired connection with higher bandwidth"

#### Hyper Cool
- "All-new active cooling system and temperature regulator"
- "Always get 100% of available torque"
- "Perfect for endurance sessions"

- "Solid as a Rock"

- "Ridgid, reliable, yet classy"

### Reviewer's Reality Check
- **Max Torque Claims**: "Max torque" is different from peak/holding torque
  - Sport (9 Nm) feels like ~4-6 Nm compared to other bases
  - Pro (18 Nm) feels like ~14-15 Nm compared to other bases
  - Similar advertising pattern to Moza
- **Zero-Cogging**: "Not an issue. It never has been an issue" with previous Alphas
- **21-Bit Encoder**: Unlikely anyone can tell difference from competitors
- **Open Ecosystem**: Excellent approach but Mag Link still required for current wheels
- **Hyper Cool**: Base does stay cool during use
- **Front Mount**: Not straightforward despite marketing claims

## Alpha Evo Lineup Overview

### Product Line Structure
- **Alpha Evo Sport**: 9 Nm entry level
- **Alpha Evo** (unnamed): 12 Nm mid-tier
- **Alpha Evo Pro**: 18 Nm flagship
- **Naming Confusion**: "Naming is a little bit confusing"
  - No longer called Mini/Ultimate
  - Middle tier has no name (just "12 Newton meter")
  - "Very nice to be able to identify them by name very easily"
  - Gets complicated when people ask "Alpha U or Alpha Mini?"
  - "Sim Magic needs to really kind of stick with this branding"

### Key Innovations
- **No Cogging**: Major marketing point (though reviewer questions necessity)
  - "Never really subscribed to much" - didn't feel cogging on older Alphas
  - "Not an issue. It never has been an issue"
  - "Big selling point, I don't know"
- **USB Pass-Through**: Through shaft (power and data)
  - "Very very interesting"
  - Only works with upcoming Zeus range wheels
  - Current wheels (GT Neo, Neo X) still need Mag Link for SimHub
- **Power Pass-Through**: Through shaft
- **Connectivity**: "Lots and lots and lots of ports" on back
  - 8 Ethernet-style ports for future devices (not for existing CAN bus devices)
  - USB connections for CAN bus (old devices)
  - Not a generic USB hub
  - "Very curious what all of those are"

### Form Factor & Design
- **Size**: "Slightly wider than it is tall"
  - Older Simagic units were "very very square"
  - "A little bit longer than it is tall"
- **Best Form Factor**: "Better than anything out there"
  - Better than Simucube, Asetek, Moza, everything
  - "Absolutely love that"
  - Nice and compact
  - "Really nice proximity to the screen"
  - "Look excellent on a rig"
  - Important consideration for reviewer
- **Magnetic Area**: For clip-on screens (seen at Dortmund Expo)
  - Magnetic area seemed peeled on review unit (transit damage)
  - "Not particularly nice there"

### Mounting Options
- **Base Mount**: Exact same pattern as previous Alphas
  - Direct bolt-on upgrade from Alpha Mini
  - "All of your existing stuff should fit here"
- **Side Mount**: Same as previous Alphas (reviewer loved this)
  - "Great wheel deck from ASR Pro"
- **Front Mount**: NOT straightforward
  - "Very disappointing in my opinion"
  - Front mount is actually a side mount with little bits on the side
  - "Complex procedure"
  - Requires bracket
  - "I love a front mounted wheelbase"
  - Reviewer prefers front mounting when not reviewing
  - "I feel like they've kind of I don't know if this bracket was entirely necessary"
- **Compatibility**: All existing Alpha mounting works

### Power Supplies
- **Sport (9 Nm)**: 252 watt, 48V
  - "Nice compact power supply for the size"
  - Standard kettle connection
  - "Different looking power supply to what we usually see"
  - By brand GVE
- **Pro (18 Nm)**: 400 watt, 48V, 8.3 amps
  - "Most compact 400 watt power supply that I've seen"
  - By brand GVE
  - Has Simagic labeling
  - "Well presented and a lot nicer than a lot of the competitors"
- **On/Off Switch**: On plug (not on unit)
  - "Never really understood it, but it's not very practical"
  - "Usually where these are, it's not very accessible on a Sim rig"
  - "Would like a power button on the unit itself"
  - "Hope they've put one of those in"
- **Power Button**: On back of unit (thankfully)
  - "Does turn on from the button on the back? Yes, it does"
  - "Delighted with that"

### Quick Release
- **Shaft Pins**: "Looks very similar to the previous devices"
- **Spring-Loaded Pins**: New feature
  - "Spring-loaded little guys here"
  - "Maybe for power or maybe just for cushioning. I don't know"
  - Sit slightly proud of unit
  - "Little bit of scope for things to rub off those things"
- **Design Evolution**: 
  - Started with similar design
  - Went to very flush design
  - Now less flush again
- **Compatibility**: "Exactly the same as far as I can see"

- **Power Delivery**: "Having power issues with that"
  - Screen comes on but not enough power
  - "Not enough power being delivered to that"
  - "Screen is coming on, but there's no power"
- **Known Issue**: "Known issue with the pre-production version"
  - "They told me that this is a known issue"

  - "Haven't tested a lot of the older ones"

  - "Upcoming Zeus range, they don't need the Mag Link cable"
- **Current Experience**: SimHub not finding devices at all
  - Tested with GT Neo and Neo X
  - "Searching device. It's not finding it"
  - "Brightness must be set to 100% in SIM Pro and the wheel must be connected through USB"

## Alpha Evo Sport (9 Nm)

### Force Feedback Performance
- **Power**: Lacks sufficient power for formula cars
  - "I'm lacking power here, guys"
  - At 100% in-game and 9 Nm: feels like ~4 Nm
  - Alpha Mini (10 Nm) felt like 11+ Nm - much more powerful
- **Detail Quality**: "Force feedback quality itself is phenomenal"
  - Details are spot on, excellent communication
  - Just needs more range/power
- **Better in iRacing**: Felt more powerful than in Assetto Corsa
- **Vibration**: Lots of vibration noise through rig
- **Mechanical Friction**: Helped make it feel more powerful
  - Don't normally like it but necessary here

### Comparison to Alpha Mini
- Alpha Mini was 10 Nm (actually 11+ Nm peak) - plenty of power
- Sport is 9 Nm - "definitely feels like 9 Newton meters peak"
- Many said reviewer got early Alpha Mini closer to 12 Nm Evo

### Final Verdict (Sport)
- **Not Recommended**: Unlike Alpha Mini recommendation
  - "Would not be recommending the Sport to people where I would have recommended the Alpha Mini highly in the past"
- **Go for Pro or 12 Nm**: "I would go for that Pro"
  - "The 12 Newton meter is going to be enough for most people"
- **Good for Beginners**: Will be plenty for beginner to intermediate
  - But will want more power eventually
- **Detail Quality**: Excellent, just lacks power range

## Alpha Evo Pro (18 Nm)

### Power Testing
- **At 9 Nm**: Feels same as Sport at 9 Nm (relatively light)
  - Two finger test: can push through corner with minimal force
  - "I don't think this is 9 Newton meters"
- **At 15 Nm**: "Much more along the lines of what I expect"
  - Still feels like 11-12 Nm (not scientific)
  - "Force feedback quality exceptional here. It's very, very good"
- **At 18 Nm**: Still not overwhelming
  - Feels like 14-15 Nm compared to other bases
  - "More than enough" for most people
  - "Abso fuckingutely, for sure"

### Force Feedback Quality
- **Exceptional**: "Top quality force feedback, guys. Make no mistake"
- **Very Direct**: "Feels very connected to the car, to the front axle"
- **Natural Grip Feel**: Can feel tire grip perfectly
- **Wheel Movement**: Reacts beautifully to road undulations
- **Detail Range**: "Beautiful range of detail"
- **Sliders**: "Seem very intuitive and simple"

### Noise Characteristics
- **Constant Noise**: "Bass does make quite a bit of noise"
- **Vibration**: "Sending a huge amount of vibration through my rig"
  - Short sharp high frequency jolts
  - "Whole rig is shaking"
  - Like a bass shaker
- **High Pitched Whine**: When going slowly, gets lower with speed
- **Comparison**: Similar to why turned off Logitech True Force

### Torque Measurement Discussion
- **"Max Torque"**: Not peak or holding - different measurement
  - Numbers seem higher than actual output
  - Similar to Moza advertising pattern
- **Comparisons**:
  - Asetek La Prima 12 Nm: would rip arms off
  - Logitech Pro 11 Nm: very powerful
  - Moza R12: nowhere near as powerful as those two
  - CSL DD with power adapter: Sport feels similar or less

### Settings Adjustments
- Reduced mechanical damper
- Increased wheel rotation speed to 100 (then back to ~60)
- Reduced feedback detail (contributes to vibration)
- Sliders very intuitive

### Final Verdict (Pro)
- **Phenomenal**: "Force feedback experience is really nice"
- **Recommended**: "I would go for that Pro"
  - "Can't wait to put that Pro on the rig"
- **Sufficient Power**: At 15-18 Nm, more than enough
- **Excellent Quality**: "Really is an excellent piece of kit"

## Force Feedback Deep Dive

### Unique Characteristics
- **Micro Vibrations**: "Feel micro vibrations, if that makes sense"
  - Extra little level of detail
  - Doesn't feel canned
  - Comes from simulator
  - "Cream on the top" that previous Alphas were missing
- **True Force Similarity**: "Similar to true force on like 10%"
  - Surprised Simagic didn't lead with this marketing
  - Instead focused on cogging (which reviewer doesn't care about)
- **Distinctive in Blind Test**: Would be able to tell it apart
  - Including from Simucube
  - Unique frequency vibration characteristics
  - "Rattling and vibration... can feel that"

### Detail Quality
- **Excellent**: Curb detail, road feel, communication
- **Road Detail**: Not massive amount
- **Curb Detail**: Excellent
- **Crests & Compressions**: Feels perfectly
- **Suspension**: Curbing feeling excellent
- **Understeer**: Nice and communicative

### Comparison to Previous Alphas
- **Better Force Feedback**: "Might be just slightly crisper"
  - Extra layer of detail
  - Puts it right up there with elite
- **Power Difference**: Alphas had more actual power
  - "Something about the power of the Alphas"
  - "My gut is saying the Alphas still have slightly better power"
- **Would Tell Apart**: In blind test due to vibration characteristics

### Top Tier Assessment
- **Elite Company**: Asetek, Logitech, Simucube, Sim Magic, VRS
- **Fanatec**: Never experienced this kind of force feedback
- **Trustmaster**: Never experienced it
- **Cammus**: Good with C5 and C12

## Overall Assessment

### Strengths
- **Form Factor**: Best in class - compact, looks great
- **Force Feedback Quality**: Top tier with unique micro vibrations
- **Detail**: Excellent curb detail, road feel, communication
- **Mounting**: Side and base mount compatibility
- **Open Ecosystem**: Third-party QR approach
- **Software**: Intuitive, live updates
- **Build Quality**: Solid, well-presented
- **Power Supplies**: Compact, well-designed

### Weaknesses
- **Power Output**: Less than advertised/expected
  - Sport too weak for serious use
  - Pro feels like 14-15 Nm not 18 Nm
- **Noise**: Constant vibration noise through rig
  - High pitched whine
  - May annoy household members
- **No Front Mount**: Complex bracket system instead
- **Mag Link Still Required**: For current wheels with SimHub
- **Third-Party QR Issues**: Pre-production power delivery problems
- **Naming Confusion**: Inconsistent product naming
- **Release Management**: Messy embargo, pre-orders before reviews

### Recommendations
- **Pro**: Excellent choice, highly recommended
  - "Phenomenal force feedback"
  - "Will not be disappointed"
- **Sport**: Not recommended (unlike Alpha Mini)
  - Go for 12 Nm or Pro instead
- **Alpha Owners**: No reason to upgrade
  - Alpha U still exceptional
  - More of a sideways move
- **New Buyers**: Evo Pro for longevity and future compatibility

### Market Position
- **Well Priced**: "Madly like well priced"
- **Good Investment**: "Yes, damn right it is"
- **Company in Transition**: Still realigning, not perfectly streamlined
- **Pressing Right Buttons**: Despite transition, doing well overall

  - Well priced
